01 — Rationale

Why a departures board, not a postcard

Most travel sites reach for the same gestalt: bright sky photography, a friendly rounded sans, a sunny yellow or coral CTA. That reads as a booking widget, not an operator you'd trust with a family trip or a corporate group. Bundelkhand Pride Travels borrows instead from the world of flight manifests, boarding passes and airport departure boards — instruments that are precise, a little formal, and quietly confident. Ink-navy grounds carry that precision; a single warm gold carries the luxury cue; itinerary days, prices and dates are set in tabular mono, like a ticket stub. The result should feel engineered, not decorated.

02 — Color

Palette

Six named colors. Ink and paper alternate as the page's "night" and "day" bands — hero, footer and key CTA moments sit on ink; browsing and reading sections sit on paper. Gold is the only accent spent on action; pine is reserved for a single semantic use — adventure/trek category tags — so it never competes with gold for attention.

Text-on-color rule: cream (#EDE7D6) on ink, ink (#0A0E1A) on gold or paper — never pure black or pure white, so every surface keeps the same warm bias.

03 — Typography

Three roles, one hierarchy

A warm italic serif carries emotion at large sizes only — headlines, pull quotes, big stat numbers. A plain system sans runs everything a user reads or operates. A monospace renders anything that is fundamentally a number or a code — prices, dates, itinerary days, route pairs — the ticket-stub motif made literal.

04 — Buttons

Buttons & actions

One filled gold button per view, maximum — everything else is outline or text. Buttons carry a verb, never "Submit" or "Click here."

10 — Motion

Principles

Duration

180–260ms for hover/press. 400–600ms for scroll-reveal. Nothing longer — this is a booking flow, not a title sequence.

Easing

ease-out for anything entering, ease-in for anything leaving. No bounce, no elastic overshoot.

Scroll reveal

Section content fades up 12px on first entry into view, once only — never re-triggers on scroll-back.

Respect for the user

prefers-reduced-motion disables all transform/opacity transitions site-wide, not just the obvious ones.

11 — Iconography

Icons

Single-weight line icons, 1.5px stroke, no fills, no emoji anywhere in the product. Icons illustrate a function (search, calendar, WhatsApp, phone) — they never stand in for a numbered step or a decorative flourish.
